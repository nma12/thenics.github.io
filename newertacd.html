OUR

PHILOSOPHY

At The Nazarite International Christian School, we believe that God is the ultimate source of all truth and knowledge, and that learning is an act that honors and glorifies Him. Guided by this belief, we challenge our students by engaging them in rigorous academics through a variety of instructional approaches that support diverse learners.

[ THE ACADEMIC SEQUENCE ]

A seamless progression from foundational learning to university-ready excellence.

The Nazarite International Christian School academic sequence uses diverse instructional approaches and rigorous curricula at different levels to ensure optimal learning.

01

Early Years

PRE-K • FOUNDATIONS

Throughout the early years (Pre-K), our emphasis is on building strong foundations. The ACE curriculum helps us accomplish this by providing structured independence, self-paced mastery, and consistent biblical integration. Biblical principles woven into daily lessons build character alongside academic skills.

02

Elementary School

GRADES 1-6 • MASTERY

At the elementary level (grades 1-6), our students continue with the individualized, mastery-based system of the ACE curriculum. They focus on building a strong foundation comprehension and following instructions.

03

Middle School

GRADES 7-8 • TRANSITION

At this critical developmental stage, our Middle schoolers (grades 7-8) are exposed to stronger, more practical Christian American curricula that emphasize application, collaboration, and deeper thinking. Students move beyond memorization to analysis, evaluation, and creative problem-solving across all subjects.

04

Junior Highschool

GRADES 9-10 • DIPLOMA TRACK

At the Junior highschool level (Grades 9-10), students continue with the same instructional model as Middle School, while diving into highschool level subjects and beginning the climb towards an American Highschool Diploma.

05

Senior Highschool

GRADES 11-12 • A LEVELS

At the Senior Highschool level (Grades 11-12) our students are prepared for university with Cambridge International A levels. A-Levels mirror university-style learning with in-depth subject mastery and independent research expectations. Finally graduating with both an American high school diploma and an A level certificate.
